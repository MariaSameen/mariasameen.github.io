About

Who I Am

I am a cyber security expert specializing in adversarial machine learning, privacy engineering, and the design of trustworthy digital systems. My work spans model poisoning and phishing detection, privacy-by-design pattern development, and IoT security/privacy labeling — translating technical risk analysis into practical safeguards for both developers and consumers. I investigate deceptive patterns and choice architecture in digital platforms, examining how interface design shapes user behaviour and exposure to harm, with research informing regulatory recommendations (e.g., for Ofcom). My FemTech research audits third-party data practices in health apps, driving calls for stronger GDPR protections. Most recently, I have applied multi-agent LLM systems to industrial control system security, evaluating AI reliability in critical infrastructure defense. My doctoral research exemplifies this interdisciplinary orientation, fusing empirical HCI methods, consumer behaviour analysis, and regulatory theory to address deceptive design as a systemic, cross-stakeholder problem. Holding a Ph.D. in Cyber Security from the Universities of Bristol and Bath, I combine rigorous empirical methods with a consistent focus on translating research into actionable, real-world impact across academia, industry, and policy.
